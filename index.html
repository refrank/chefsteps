Skills

My skills map to your needs.

Be Humble, Be Smart, Be Fast

I work quickly, efficiently and try to live by the quote, "Humility is not thinking less of oneself. It's thinking of oneself less."

Strong Portfolio of Designs

My body of work ranges from a nationwide government software project to a small bootstrapped startup. You can see some of it here.

Ownership of Product & Deep Design Knowledge

I dig into what I do. I've worn many hats including Product Owner, Analyst, and UX Designer. I've completed courses on the Fundamentals of Design, Human Computer Interaction and a Comprehensive Guide to Usability/UX.

Clear Communicator, Both Written and Verbal

In my last career as a technical business analyst it was quite literally my job to be a clear communicator. I was the liaison between developers, business clients, stakeholders and users. Each group has their own language, jargon and communication style. It was my job to translate and bridge the gap.

Strong Visual Design Aesthetic and Taste Level

Design is in my blood. My grandfather gave me regular art and design lessons starting at age 2. I won numerous art/design awards in high school. I'm constantly creating both physical and digital projects for fun and for work. I love the intersection of deciding what should be built, how it should work and what it should look like.

Whiteboarding, Sketching and Quick to Iterate

Rapid paper prototyping is one of my favorite techniques for iterating designs. I'm also a whiteboard junky so much so that I bought a jumbo pack of dry erase markers with my own money when I worked at the Veterans Affairs.

Ability to Prototype or Build in HTML/CSS/Javascript

So with full disclosure I'm fairly new to HTML/CSS, though I've done a couple small projects using it, including this one. I'm comfortable making mockups and prototypes in Balsamiq, UXPin and Protoshare to name a few. The bit of coding I've done whetted my appetite. I always push myself to learn new things so if this is how you need your prototypes built, I'll be sure to come up to speed quickly.

Experience Running and Optimizing Experiments

I have experience running experiments both in the technology field and in the real world. I've conducted informal user testing, run A/B tests, and conducted a year long experiment studying prices and consumer behaviour at a coffee shop I ran during college.

Lots of Stuff Shipped

I know how to get things done on time. For three years I worked on a web app for the U.S. Veterans Affairs and we shipped many different iterative releases. I also run my own recipe site, Veritably Veg, and help my fiancé with his startup, SightReadingMastery. I'm intimately familiar with getting stuff shipped and out the door, not letting perfect be the enemy of the good, while not putting out something you're not proud of.

Usability/UX Research Experience

UX is my jam. I love getting into the shoes of users. I was the voice of reason who lead my previous team to a more user-centred design approach. I'm a big data nerd, love crunching feedback from users and applying that towards bettering the product design. I could go on for a while on this topic, but I'll spare you.

Marketing/Branding Experience

When I was in college and still figuring out what I wanted to do in life I had two formative internships. One of them was working at a major advertising firm as a brand management intern for The Home Depot. I also worked at Food & Water Watch, a consumer advocacy non-profit. This gave me a good foundation in knowing how to get a message out there and the importance of staying true to brand identity to build brand recognition.

Immense Love of Food, Curiosity & Passion for Learning and Teaching

When I was 12 my parents got me an Alton Brown cookbook. It was my favorite Christmas present. When I was a teenager I would stay home and teach myself to cook instead of going to parties. I fell asleep reading books on the science and chemistry of cooking. Food is more than a lifelong passion for me; it's a way of life. I love pushing my boundaries, expanding my repertoire, and sharing what I've learned with others. It's who I am.

About

I think our personalities jive and that we can achieve great things together.

  • Born and Raised

    A Hands On Tinkerer at Heart

    I'm was raised a Montessori kid, which means I learn by doing. I try new things, I tinker, I might fail but I persevere, analyse and learn from it. Creative problem solving is my strong suit and like any cook knows, the ability to improvise when something is wrong is par for the course. I do this in the kitchen and when I'm working on websites and products.

  • Formal Education

    From Economics to Latte Champ

    I studied economics in college. It piqued my interest because it boiled down to learning about decision-making, people's habits and the large-scale trends that result from them. Outside the classroom, I ran a coffee shop, working practically full-time while a full-time student. I learned all the ins and outs of business ownership, process improvement, management and so much more. Oh and I was trained to pull shots and steam milk by a World Champion Barista.

  • Entrepreneurial Days

    My Plant-Based Website

    Post graduation I started my own online business, a recipe site called Veritably Veg. I created all my own recipes and content, jumped feet first into food photography, figured out SEO and the hard truths of starting a small business. Though it's not perfect or necessarily how I would do it today, I learned loads from this experience. And one thing's for sure, it solidified my love of creating and sharing food.

  • Foray into Software

    Texas, Y'all

    I moved to Austin, Texas to work on a large government software project. My team took a decades old pen-and-paper process and made a web app to be released nationwide. Our users were folks who didn't know the first thing about computers and thus my passion for building simple, straightforward products with lots of empathy for users was born.

  • Adventures in Asia

    Recharge Batteries. Check.
    Learn Asian Cuisine. Check.
    Expand Mind. Check.

    After several years working and saving, my fiancé and I decided to take a little break to see the world. We went to India, Thailand, Cambodia and Japan. Ate tons of good food. Took cooking classes in each country and just generally had a mind-expanding, heart-warming, recharging experience. When we got back, we moved to Seattle and I'm excited and ready to throw myself into new projects.
